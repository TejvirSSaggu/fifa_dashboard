How it works

Live refresh: the button pulls current standings and the remaining schedule straight from ESPN's public World Cup feed, then rebuilds everything. It also runs automatically each time you open the file, so opening it once a day keeps it current. If the network is blocked (some in-app previews sandbox external requests), it falls back to the last saved data and you can still log results by hand.

For each team it simulates every remaining result in its group and asks of its next match: if this team loses, can it still reach the top two? — Must win (a draw also eliminates) · Must not lose (a draw survives) · On the brink (top-two gone on a loss, best-third still open) · Stakes · Through · Out. A team is retired once it can't reach the top two and its points ceiling is ≤3; a group settles once every team is through or out.

Models a top-two finish; 8 of 12 third-placed teams also advance, so a red flag means top-two danger, not certain elimination, except where the points ceiling is already ≤3. Data: ESPN public API (unofficial) — for personal use; if it ever changes shape the refresh may fail and you can fall back to manual logging. Live refresh works best when you download the file and open it in your browser. Times shown in your local timezone.
